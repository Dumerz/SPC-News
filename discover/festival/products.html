Products of San Pablo City

Coconut

Coconut
Tree of Life

Coconut is the primary product of San Pablo. And before San Pablo is known as the Queen city of the Coconuts. All parts of the coconut is used to produce goods and products that can be use for daily living. From our trees we are able to produce oil, coco water, handicrafts and cocolumbers not only in the Philippines but also the rest of the world. Almost 4200 hectares of the land of the city is plantation of coconuts, and it can be said as the most crop for agriculture in San Pablo, and number one plant that is source of income of the city.

Rambutan

Rambutan
Scientific Name: Nephelium lappaceum

Rambutan is native to Malaysia but can be grown in most places in the Philippines including San Pablo City Laguna. SPC is suitable for those seeking opportunities in agriculture as exemplified by the barangays planted with coconut, vegetables, and fruit trees like Lanzones and Rambutan. Harvesting time of rambutan is from July to September, and this 2012 almost 1,281.57 metric tons of rambutan was produced by San Pablo, making it as one of the industry of the city.

Tilapia

Tilapia
One of table fish of Filipinos

Tilapias have laterally flattened bodies that range from about 10 to 30 cm (about 4 to 12 in) in length. They feed on a wide variety of food, including insect larvae, crustaceans, juvenile fish, worms, various plants, and detritus. Some species can survive in waters with oxygen concentrations as low as 0.1 parts per million, and one species inhabits hot springs with water temperatures as high as 40° C (104° F). However, most species of tilapia are not so tolerant.

Tilapia became a part of Filipinos table aside from galunggong, because of the fact that its a fresh water fish. It is also delicious and easy to prepare. It is easy to preservve and it takes a long time before it cant be eaten.

Lanzones

Lanzones
A small fruit with yellow and sour-sweet taste.

If you find rambutan on fruit stands along road sides of San Pablo City. You will definitely see this fruit. A yellow small fruit with sour and sweet taste that will give a citrus flavor. And is good to taste.

These fruits are freshly picked from small trees with straight trunks and can only bear abundantly when it is planted in a cool place away from the very hot sun. It is definitely good to plant this plant with Coconuts becuase the coconut can give it shade and protection against the ray of the sun.

Lanzones also become an industry in San PAblo because alot of tourist from the south and north find these frenzy fruits that are sold in fruit stand in the rural barangays along the national road.
